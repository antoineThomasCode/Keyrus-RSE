Keyrus CSR
Strategy

In 2011, Keyrus signed up to the United Nations Global Compact. Gradually, we have put in place governance to anchor ourselves in sustainable development: creation of the Keyrus Foundation in 2017, implementation of the CSR management in 2020 to steer a CSR strategy on our 3 ESG pillars.​

Environmental

GHG management, IT equipment life cycle, responsible digital and waste/recycling management.​

Social

Employee training, health and quality of life at work, ensuring gender equality and guaranteeing diversity and inclusion.​

Governance

Customer satisfaction, cybersecurity, ethics and compliance, and including CSR in all levels of business​

Through its expertise and digital partnerships, and in line with this commitment, Keyrus offers to support its customers in 2 main areas ​

CSR performance management​

At Keyrus, we believe that data is an indisputable key to business success. When used intelligently, it opens up unique opportunities to address current and future challenges. At Keyrus, we enable organizations to deploy the capabilities they need to make data matter: we apply data science at the enterprise level.​ In line with its commitment to a sustainable economy, Keyrus places particular emphasis on helping companies to manage their CSR performance. This necessarily involves a new approach to data collection, the definition of coherent KPIs, standardization of the data collected, and so on. The integration of non-financial data with financial data is a real lever for value creation, opening up a whole new world of possibilities for optimizing corporate performance.

Decarbonization

Decarbonization is a key challenge in the fight against climate change. But it is also an asset for improving competitiveness: act and control greenhouse gas emissions, then identify opportunities for improving energy efficiency and implement long-term reduction strategies, thus transforming climate challenges into opportunities for sustainable growth. ​ Keyrus responds to this environmental challenge with offers that identify and collect data to build a credible reduction plan as part of a continuous improvement approach.
